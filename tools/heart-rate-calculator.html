Training Recommendations

About Heart Rate Training

Heart rate training zones help you optimize your workouts by training at the right intensity for your goals.

Calculation Methods

  • Karvonen Method: Uses heart rate reserve (Max HR - Resting HR) for more accurate zones
  • Age-Predicted Method: Simple formula (220 - age) but less accurate

Zone Benefits

  • Zone 1 (50-60%): Active recovery, warm-up, cool-down
  • Zone 2 (60-70%): Base building, fat burning, endurance
  • Zone 3 (70-80%): Aerobic fitness, cardiovascular improvement
  • Zone 4 (80-90%): Lactate threshold, race pace training
  • Zone 5 (90-100%): Maximum effort, HIIT, sprint training

Measuring Your Heart Rate

  • Manual: Count beats for 30 seconds and multiply by 2
  • Chest Strap: Most accurate for training
  • Wrist Monitor: Convenient but less accurate during intense exercise
  • Fitness Trackers: Good for general monitoring

Safety Considerations

  • Consult a doctor before starting intense training
  • Listen to your body and don't exceed comfortable limits
  • Stop exercising if you feel dizzy, chest pain, or unusual discomfort
  • Stay hydrated and properly fueled during workouts
