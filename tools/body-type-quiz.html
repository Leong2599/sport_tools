Understanding Somatotypes

Somatotypes are a classification system for human body types, developed by Dr. William Sheldon in the 1940s. While not a definitive science, understanding your dominant somatotype can offer general insights into your metabolic tendencies and help tailor your fitness and nutrition strategies.

The Three Main Body Types

  • Ectomorph: Characterized by a lean, delicate build with small bone structure and fast metabolism. Ectomorphs often struggle to gain weight and muscle.
  • Mesomorph: Naturally athletic, strong, and muscular with a medium bone structure. Mesomorphs typically gain muscle and lose fat relatively easily.
  • Endomorph: Tends to have a larger bone structure, higher body fat percentage, and a slower metabolism. Endomorphs find it easier to gain both muscle and fat.

Important Considerations

Most people are a blend of these three somatotypes, with one usually being more dominant. This quiz is a general guide and should not be taken as medical advice. Individual results can vary based on genetics, lifestyle, and other factors. A balanced approach to diet and exercise is crucial for all body types.
